# Motivation

## Who I am
I’m an undergraduate student pursuing a Bachelor’s degree in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning at BMS Institute of Technology and Management in Bengaluru. My work so far has focused on building intelligent systems using machine learning and agent-based architectures. Through internships and personal projects, I’ve worked on areas like speech recognition, LLM-based agents, and deep learning models. I enjoy understanding complex systems by experimenting with them, debugging issues, and improving their architecture, which is what led me to explore Mesa.

## Why Mesa
I first discovered Mesa while looking for frameworks to experiment with agent-based modeling and multi-agent systems. I was particularly interested in how agent behaviors can lead to complex emergent patterns at the system level. While exploring Mesa’s codebase and examples, I became curious about the meta-agents module and began experimenting with it directly. Running the official examples and building my own small model helped me understand both the strengths of the framework and the areas where the current implementation could be improved. That hands-on exploration is what motivated me to contribute.

## What I want to learn
Through contributing to Mesa, I want to deepen my understanding of agent-based modeling and the design of simulation frameworks. I’m especially interested in learning how large open-source systems evolve over time and how architectural decisions affect usability and extensibility. I also want to improve my ability to design clean APIs, reason about system lifecycles, and collaborate with experienced maintainers on real-world software.

## Where I want to go
My goal is to become a strong open-source contributor and engineer who can work on complex systems and research-oriented software. Contributing to Mesa is an opportunity for me to learn from a community that is actively advancing agent-based modeling tools. In the long term, I hope to contribute features, examples, and improvements that make Mesa easier for researchers and developers to use, while continuing to build models and explore new applications of agent-based systems.
